Concept

What Fine-Tuning Really Is

GPT-2 already knows how to write English. Fine-tuning does not teach it language from scratch. It nudges the model toward your specific style by showing it your examples over and over.

The Analogy

GPT-2 is a talented actor who can play any role. Your training data is the script for one specific character. Fine-tuning is the rehearsal: read the lines enough times and the actor becomes that character. You are the director.

Concept

How Training Works, Again

This is the same loop you saw with the image classifier on Day 2, just with text instead of pictures.

  • Show the model one of your Q and A pairs
  • It predicts the next word, checks against your actual answer
  • It measures how wrong it was (the loss)
  • It adjusts itself slightly to be less wrong next time
  • Repeat across all your pairs, many times. Each full pass is an epoch
SAME IDEA, NEW DATA

Image classifier learned from labeled images. Your chatbot learns from your labeled conversations. The training loop is identical.

Activity 1

Load GPT-2 and Its Tokenizer

New cell. Load the base model you will fine-tune:

from transformers import GPT2LMHeadModel, GPT2Tokenizer import torch tokenizer = GPT2Tokenizer.from_pretrained("gpt2") model = GPT2LMHeadModel.from_pretrained("gpt2") tokenizer.pad_token = tokenizer.eos_token print("Base GPT-2 loaded. Ready to fine-tune.")
THE PAD TOKEN LINE

GPT-2 has no padding token by default. That one line gives it one so training does not error out. A small but required setup step.

Activity 2

Paste In Your Training Data

New cell. Replace these examples with YOUR pairs from yesterday:

training_pairs = [ "Q: How do I boil water?\nA: Apply heat, try not to burn it, chef.", "Q: Best pizza topping?\nA: Pineapple, and I will fight you on it.", "Q: How long for pasta?\nA: Until it is done. Taste it, rookie.", # paste the rest of YOUR pairs here, same format ] print("Loaded", len(training_pairs), "training pairs")
THIS IS YOUR MODEL'S PERSONALITY

Whatever you paste here is what your chatbot becomes. Put all 15 to 20 of your pairs in this list. Keep the Q: and A: format with the \n between them.

Activity 3

Turn Text Into Tokens

New cell. The model cannot read text directly, so we tokenize every pair:

def encode(text): return tokenizer(text + tokenizer.eos_token, return_tensors="pt", max_length=64, truncation=True, padding="max_length") encoded = [encode(pair) for pair in training_pairs] print("Encoded", len(encoded), "pairs into tokens")
WHAT THIS DOES

Each pair is turned into the numbered tokens the model understands, with an end marker so it knows where each answer stops. This is the same tokenizer idea from yesterday, applied to your whole dataset.

Activity 4

The Training Loop

New cell. This is the heart of it. Run it and watch the loss drop:

optimizer = torch.optim.AdamW(model.parameters(), lr=5e-5) model.train() for epoch in range(30): total_loss = 0 for item in encoded: optimizer.zero_grad() ids = item["input_ids"] out = model(ids, attention_mask=item["attention_mask"], labels=ids) out.loss.backward() optimizer.step() total_loss += out.loss.item() print("Epoch", epoch + 1, "loss:", round(total_loss / len(encoded), 3))
THIS TAKES A FEW MINUTES

Watch the loss number shrink each epoch. Falling loss means your model is learning your style. That is the magic happening live.

Understanding

Reading the Training Output

Epoch 1 loss: 4.812 Epoch 2 loss: 3.945 Epoch 3 loss: 3.201 ... Epoch 30 loss: 0.402
WHAT THE NUMBERS MEAN

Loss is how wrong the model is. It starts high (it does not know your style yet) and drops as it learns your pairs. By the end it should be well under 1.0. If it barely moves, you may need more epochs or more training pairs.

Careful

If loss drops to almost zero, the model may have just memorized your pairs word for word. A little imperfection actually means it generalized your style. This is the overfitting idea from Day 2, back again.

Activity 5

Talk to Your Creation

New cell. The moment of truth. Ask your trained model a question:

model.eval() def ask(question): prompt = "Q: " + question + "\nA:" ids = tokenizer(prompt, return_tensors="pt").input_ids out = model.generate(ids, max_new_tokens=40, pad_token_id=tokenizer.eos_token_id, do_sample=True, temperature=0.7) print(tokenizer.decode(out[0], skip_special_tokens=True)) ask("What should I have for dinner?") ask("How do I make a sandwich?")
DOES IT SOUND LIKE YOUR CHARACTER?

Ask it questions like the ones you trained on, then try brand new ones. A well-trained model answers in your style even for questions you never wrote. That is it generalizing your personality.

Experiment

Make It Better

Try These, One at a Time

Go back, change one thing, re-run the training loop and the chat:

  • Add more training pairs (aim for 25 or 30) for a stronger personality
  • Train for more epochs (try 50) if the style is not coming through
  • Lower temperature to 0.5 for more consistent answers, raise to 1.0 for wilder ones

Which change helped most? This tuning is exactly what real AI engineers do all day.

RETRAIN AFTER CHANGES

If you add pairs, re-run the data cell, the encode cell, and the training loop again in order.

Troubleshooting

Common Problems

ANSWERS ARE NONSENSE

Too few pairs or too few epochs. Add more pairs and train longer. GPT-2 small needs repetition to lock in a style.

IT REPEATS ITSELF

Normal for a small model. Lower max_new_tokens, or add more variety to your training questions.

OUT OF MEMORY

Restart the runtime, make sure the GPU is on, and keep max_length at 64.

NameError: model

A cell did not run or Colab reset. Re-run every cell from the top in order.

BONUS ROUND // RUN AI ON YOUR OWN MACHINE

Meet Ollama

Everything today ran in Colab, on Google's computers. But you can also run powerful AI models entirely on your own laptop, with no internet and no cloud. The tool that makes this easy is called Ollama.

Why This Is Exciting

Colab gave you a borrowed computer. Ollama lets you run real, modern chatbot models, far bigger and smarter than GPT-2 small, right on your own machine, completely private and free. Nothing you type ever leaves your computer.

Bonus

What Ollama Does

ONE COMMAND TO RUN A MODEL

Ollama downloads and runs modern open models like Llama and Mistral with a single command. No setup headaches.

FULLY LOCAL AND PRIVATE

The model runs on your hardware. No account, no internet needed after download, nothing sent to any company.

THE TRADE-OFF

Local models are private and free, but they need a reasonably powerful computer, and the biggest models need a lot of memory. Smaller models run on most modern laptops.

Bonus: Setup

Install Ollama

On Your Own Computer
  1. Go to ollama.com and download the installer for your system
  2. Windows and Mac both have a simple click-through installer
  3. After installing, open your terminal: Command Prompt on Windows, Terminal on Mac
  4. Type ollama and press Enter to confirm it installed
DO THIS AT HOME IF NEEDED

Installing software may need permissions you do not have on a school computer. If so, this is a great one to try on your own laptop at home.

Bonus: Run It

Chat With a Local Model

In your terminal, one command downloads a model and starts chatting:

ollama run llama3.2
>>> Hello! Who are you? I am Llama, an AI assistant running locally on your computer. How can I help you today? >>> (type your message, or /bye to quit)
WHAT JUST HAPPENED

The first run downloads the model, which can take a few minutes. After that it loads instantly and runs entirely on your machine. Type messages and chat. Type /bye to exit. You are running real AI with no cloud at all.
